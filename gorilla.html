GORILLA

Gorillas (genus Gorilla) are the largest living primates, sharing 98.3% of their DNA with humans. Our western lowland gorillas are generally gentle giants despite their impressive size and strength.

These intelligent creatures live in tight-knit family groups led by a dominant silverback male. They communicate through vocalizations, body language, and even facial expressions similar to humans.

Gorillas are primarily herbivorous, eating up to 40 pounds of vegetation daily. They build nests each night from branches and leaves, showing remarkable planning skills.
