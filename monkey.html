MONKEY

Our squirrel monkeys (Saimiri sciureus) are small, agile primates from South America's tropical forests. These highly social creatures live in troops of up to 500 individuals in the wild.

Squirrel monkeys have the largest brain-to-body mass ratio of all primates. They communicate through over 25 distinct vocalizations and use their tails for balance (though not for grasping).

These omnivores eat fruits, insects, and small vertebrates. Their yellow legs and white faces make them one of the most colorful monkey species.
