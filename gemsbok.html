GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope native to the arid regions of Southern Africa. With their striking black and white facial markings and long, straight horns, they're one of the most recognizable desert animals.

These magnificent creatures can survive without water for extended periods, getting moisture from the plants they eat. Their light-colored coats reflect sunlight, while specialized blood vessels in their noses cool incoming air.

Gemsbok can defend themselves against predators like lions by using their rapier-like horns which can grow up to 33 inches (85 cm) long. They typically live in herds of 10-40 animals.
