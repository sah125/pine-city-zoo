KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. Despite common nicknames, they're not bears - their closest living relative is actually the wombat!

Koalas sleep 18-20 hours per day due to their low-energy eucalyptus leaf diet. They have special gut bacteria to detoxify the poisonous leaves, and fingerprints remarkably similar to humans.

Baby koalas (joeys) live in their mother's pouch for 6-7 months. Our koalas are part of an international conservation breeding program.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

Our koala habitat is located at enclosure M8 in the Australian Walkabout zone, complete with eucalyptus trees.
